Family Tradition Since 2004

Ashley Limousin Farms began over 20 years ago with a simple vision: raise the highest quality beef using traditional farming methods and modern animal husbandry practices. What started as a small operation has grown into a trusted source for premium, farm-fresh beef.

We specialize in Limousin and Angus cattle, two breeds celebrated for producing exceptional beef. Limousin cattle are known for their lean, heavily muscled carcasses and low fat content, while Angus cattle contribute rich marbling and unparalleled tenderness. Together, they create beef that's second to none.

Every animal on our farm is raised with care and respect. They graze on lush pastures, receive individual attention, and are never given growth hormones. This commitment to quality and animal welfare is what makes our beef truly special.

Originating from France, Limousin cattle are prized worldwide for their superior carcass quality and efficient meat production.

  • Exceptional lean-to-fat ratio
  • High percentage of premium cuts
  • Superior muscle development
  • Excellent feed efficiency
  • Renowned for tenderness and flavor
  • Lower cholesterol content

How to Order

Getting farm-fresh beef is easy. Here's how it works:

Step 1: Contact Us

Call, email, or fill out our contact form to reserve your beef. We'll answer all your questions and confirm availability. Current wait times vary by season.

Step 2: Choose Your Cuts

We'll send you a detailed cut sheet where you specify exactly how you want your beef processed‚Äîsteak thickness, roast sizes, how much ground beef, and any special requests.

Step 3: Deposit & Processing

A deposit (typically 25%) reserves your beef. Final payment is based on hanging weight and is due at pickup. Processing fees are separate and paid directly to the processor.

Step 4: Pickup or Delivery

Your beef will be ready for pickup at our farm or the processing facility (typically 2-3 weeks after processing). Local delivery may be available‚Äîask us for details.

Understanding Hanging Weight

Hanging weight is the weight of the carcass after initial processing. You'll receive approximately 60-65% of the hanging weight as final packaged beef. For example, a 400 lb hanging weight yields about 240-260 lbs of beef for your freezer.
